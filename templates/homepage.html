    NYC Waste Fast Facts

    12,000 Tons

    of waste are produced daily in New York City, despite the fact that New York City holds no landfills or incinerators. Much of the non-recyclable waste is sent to landfills in Pennsylvania, Ohio, & Virginia's landfills.

    14,000,0000 Tons

    of waste are produced annually in New York City, which is around 10 million tons more than Chicago, 9 million tons more than Boston, & 12 million more than Philadephia.

    7,800,000 Miles

    are ran by Diesel trucks carrying solely Manhattan's garbage annually. This is equivalent to driving over 312 times around the Earth!

    Project Motive

    The first step to solving a problem is to identify & acknowledge it. We want our project to be purposeful, educational, and insightful. We want people to utilize our website to learn more about New York City's historical waste trends and for them to ultimately realize how big of a waste management problem this city holds. We want people to start realizing that this is a problem of great urgency, and a problem that can be majorly alleviated if they start to care and create change.

    Preventing excessive waste benefits you, your community, and the environmental as a whole.

    Many waste prevention efforts often simply involve a change in attitude or lifestyle; you can make waste prevention a part of your daily routine. A little time and effort goes a long way toward bettering the environment overall.

    • It's now or never.
    • Start learning about current waste management issues & unlearning bad waste disposal habits.
    • Check out our website to learn about waste trends in NYC as a whole, by boroughs, and locate where you can responsibly drop-off special waste personally.
