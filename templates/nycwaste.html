Quick Facts

1. New York City produces more than 14 million tons of trash every year.


2. NYC currently holds a budget of $1.83 billion towards the city's sanitation.


3. NYC held a 2015 goal of sending 0 waste to landfills by 2030. Currently, it is nowhere near that goal.


4. NYC held a goal of reaching a 33% waste diversion rate, but is struggling to reach it with a current diversion rate of 17.6%


5. Department of Sanitation, New York (DSNY) handles about 3.8 million tons of trash annually, or an average of about 10,000 tons daily.


6. NYC residents throw out 400,000 tons of paper every year.
