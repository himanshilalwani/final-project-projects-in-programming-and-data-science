In Terms of Recycling:

Brooklyn overall had the fourth highest diversion rate out of all 5 NYC boroughs. In other words, Brookyln, with an overall 16.5% diversion rate, held the fourth highest level of properly recycled total waste out of all 5 boroughs. The rather low recycling rates in Brooklyn most definitely can be attributed to how populous the borough is.

In Terms of Waste:

The largest amount of waste over time amongst all 5 NYC boroughs has been collected from Brooklyn. Yearly total waste levels sharply and steadily increased since 1990 to 1995 and remained at significantly high levels onwards. Generally, Brooklyn's yearly total waste ranged from 600,000 to 1,100,000 tons, peaking in 2021. Out of the 3 types of waste, refuse waste was the most collected amongst the sub-categories, peaking in around 1992, 2003, & 2021.

In Terms of Cleanliness:

Brooklyn overall was the least clean out of all 5 NYC boroughs, with acceptable streets levels over time ranging from around 85 to 95%. It experienced many ups & downs throughout the years, with the lowest in 2022 at around 85% and highest in around 2007 at 95%. What seems to be concerning about Brooklyn is how the cleanliness level of streets seem to be dropping over time since around 2016 to today. This is likely because Brooklyn is highly populated.
